Odyssey - Flat Field Subtraction

Noise in camera images is of two different kinds. One is spatial noise, the other is temporal noise. The former depends on the inhomogeneities in pixel response, and is very well reproducible. The latter is completely unpredictable, and cannot be eliminated in images taken on-the-fly, but needs time averages.

Flat field subtraction consists in subtracting a compensation image from the data continuously sent by the camera. However, the only way to obtain this compensation image is to sample the camera output. In order to reduce the temporal noise, several images should be averaged. The flat field compensation can also recover small lighting inhomogeneities.

The compensation image can be supplied by an external program, but the Odyssey module already contains a utility for this purpose.
