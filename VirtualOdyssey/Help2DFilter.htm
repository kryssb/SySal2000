Odyssey - 2d Filtering

Real-life images usually show long-range lighting variations and shadows; stopping electrons produce crowds of black spots. Grains usually have a very tight acceptable size range. These are all good reasons not to use a simple gray level threshold to select the dark pixels that belong to grains.

2d FIR (Finite Impulse Response) digital filters can help very much. They can enhance the grain images and suppress the background. A 2d FIR filter is simply obtained by convolving a fixed matrix (called kernel) with the image gray level. Odyssey conventions require that meaningful pixels be high and background pixels be low. The image is then binarized by setting a threshold (cutoff) on the filter output rather than on the gray level.
When detecting large objects such as calibration marks, filters are almost useless. In that case, the simple gray level threshold behaviour must be restored. This is simply obtained by setting up a 1x1 filter, with a single coefficient = -1. In that case, the cutoff must be set = -(desired gray threshold).
